# Late Night Seoul: Where to Drink

Seoul's best drinking happens after midnight—and if you're following a tourist itinerary to bed by 10pm, you're missing the version of this city that locals actually live in. These four spots aren't pulled from a "best of" roundup. They're the kind of places with regulars, opinions, and food worth eating alongside your drinks. Two are bars that take their food seriously. One is a teppanyaki counter that happens to be excellent for lingering. One does natural wine in a room that feels like someone's very good apartment. All of them are worth the trip, and one actually stays open until 2am.

---

## 1. Cheolpannam — Teppanyaki Counter, Drinks That Don't Require Ordering a Meal

![](https://places.googleapis.com/v1/places/ChIJNfLmZJaifDURfZNZGgzVc2c/photos/AU_ZVEHrDMPWahLLDKTBcw3rXSKOqu0zYw-RtwqVNjGfzL-uL4k9To-pjX87FdlYXOoYCjwlOrkV-h4EfWWJ5Ab6RzksZE063T7ObbvVuD8YSIbukvLgT0NXvQuVpFHgy9Sn6gcuZcF6aALBOoOsFA91s3sFhVi-tKDDEkQ3OqbZfFmSjkG6iJCRRCe2v_Oo6rAouMLrb2xsO3yknUAvc8Cj3NUCoRmWMxipY-LBg1hrN5g8qMlh-hZBX8yrOd52guKUriJdiv5ll6qJi_6NknXqcf3zh8GAVe8H1qNTE6PveDj1rGqm2orxRNODgG2M61RReDN1AouGDLpvJne6ZsOE9cV47GbojeRW1J1CPwaYMNPtoZLaXf7G3XGxWw8pXi3KbvgQvdYPS-NVpuuMf7YgkiVi2AAP9N2HmC7ZKuOe577MNQ/media?maxWidthPx=800&key=AIzaSyBE2cwdc_b0H934UaYurklIc7toYDXhGGQ)


If you want to sit at a counter, watch someone cook in front of you, and drink at a pace that doesn't feel rushed, Cheolpannam is the answer for Jongno. It's a teppanyaki bar in the Japanese izakaya style—a flat iron griddle, a skilled cook, and a setup that makes the whole thing feel like a performance without being precious about it. The cook has been at this counter long enough that regulars know their order before they sit down. The wagyu comes out grilled right in front of you and it's genuinely good, not just expensive. The salmon sashimi is fresh and handled properly. If you're hungry, the yakisoba finished on the hot plate is the move. This is not a party bar—it's a counter where you settle in, order a few rounds, and actually talk.

**What to order:** Wagyu, salmon sashimi, yakisoba
**Price:** $$, roughly 60,000–90,000 KRW per person for drinks and food
**Getting there:** Gwanghwamun Station, 13-minute walk
**Hours:** Mon–Sat, 5pm–12am. Closed Sunday.
**More info:** [bapmap.com/spots/cheolpannam](https://bapmap.com/spots/cheolpannam)

## 2. Mutin — Natural Wine Bar in Hannam-dong, French Food Done Right

![](https://places.googleapis.com/v1/places/ChIJg0V3MpmjfDURwSP_uQOyG1I/photos/AU_ZVEEMxRoh00V-nPiKO8HrvdBCosy25cbiywyxAkg5-HdgD2nnt2bSV8sUMLtXnaJ2b9ZNA9ixR-a26BqQomP0bgSNhd4IrbscUkZ2wTPQRYZt4vL7VNdGFwGe-qJbhheZX1LXYNe4uESVMvXUNxxwnkrCFa11TUKY9zD7HFLrkE0PJo866Z5HApzWE6tP3FP0FTmhDaYH6vwa31WfzB0_l0GpRrV16Gef0JxGHiYzL91Hm16AcFbMjCyDSYrGq0Wnwv0uXwcFleVOjU7Ol3rPaFwFPriZF5e93bMdzWkQ1kqUBA/media?maxWidthPx=800&key=AIzaSyBE2cwdc_b0H934UaYurklIc7toYDXhGGQ)


Mutin has the kind of reputation that travels by word of mouth rather than algorithm, which is usually the better signal. It's a natural wine bar on the second floor in Hannam-dong, with a French-leaning food menu and a sommelier who actually knows what they're talking about—and will steer you toward lesser-known producers rather than the obvious choices. If you're not sure what to order, just ask. That's not a throwaway suggestion; it genuinely works here. The truffle French toast with black truffle grated tableside is the dish people mention. The small plates are light, built for sharing, and made with enough technique that the food holds its own against the wine rather than just filling space. Korean celebrities have name-dropped this place publicly, which matters less than the fact that the room is consistently full of people who came back.

**What to order:** Truffle French toast, natural wine pairing (ask the sommelier), French small plates
**Price:** Budget 100,000–180,000 KRW per person for a proper wine bar night with food
**Getting there:** Itaewon Station, 10-minute walk
**Hours:** Mon–Sat, 6pm–12am. Closed Sunday.
**More info:** [bapmap.com/spots/mutin](https://bapmap.com/spots/mutin)

## 3. Sake Shop — Aged Sashimi and Sake in Songpa, Staying Open Until 2am

![](https://places.googleapis.com/v1/places/ChIJ16qr63WlfDURi4s8-4hzYEQ/photos/AU_ZVEGT1p9C0mb8JIv9GHtHwPz0EmcNm2WyC_yWhj8x87VZ5QwAly8SVhi5sM7zm0gs23RwKR-lo2V-9GWaL8R0xRceNW8NJbPJE-JoDSJv89AFdQ8VHfwQld9_rXlDAlSdwFIaBVnsU34cUS8tDoEWzBnXVztpCuH6ePgWm7JcsLR-122dl4zKpXLH3UAbnQVumH4HbxNDM7tXZ0KVc_txApTVPOV7rvGpzrEBFJkHFOoVZVGPG3l3Dlro8GRmhPaM0_p7vuKSKaLT9Xg2KMHHMdQATSNuezJlGGMsHsaL6LqnRU5e7aRjK0SYdBIwt8KM3oOTI-E3Wn_M8n6ExhyrjbErn5AB4pkbsFbwYCw_wQOhiLfr9Kd550DqYf_t9mkhfCdbLM1BmcfCU6gZiGshB6rR8U1JodUgUqHupu9cqhcdYGZ8DS_BrQbpoYuowpOU/media?maxWidthPx=800&key=AIzaSyBE2cwdc_b0H934UaYurklIc7toYDXhGGQ)


Sake Shop is the one on this list that actually keeps late-night hours, which already makes it useful. It's tucked into Songnidan-gil in Songpa, small and dark in the way a good izakaya should be, and it is specifically serious about aged sashimi. That's the thing to understand going in—the fish is aged intentionally to develop umami, served with barley miso, and it's not the same as regular sashimi. The owner sources fish from specific suppliers and ages it for precise periods to build flavor complexity. The torched mackerel with seaweed and bean paste accents is the other order worth making. The sake selection exists to match the food, not the other way around. If you find yourself on the east side of the Han after midnight and want somewhere that takes what's in your glass seriously, this is the spot.

**What to order:** Aged sashimi platter, torched mackerel
**Price:** Budget 90,000–160,000 KRW per person for sake bar pricing
**Getting there:** Jamsil Station, 13-minute walk
**Hours:** Tue–Sun, 6pm–2am. Closed Monday.
**More info:** [bapmap.com/spots/sake-shop](https://bapmap.com/spots/sake-shop)

## 4. Komfy — Wine Bar in Hapjeong, Three Minutes from the Station

![](https://places.googleapis.com/v1/places/ChIJgYIDkZ-ZfDURHFPV4jMirN0/photos/AU_ZVEGuCkNwrrWFPy3qmypYKKCy59ADxVRLHYu5HCR4uw3dNBh75wf8qP_Cv9fztrDlh1A6JG6IqYrCC8kmEQtXJgqZ27x484l44VgQdVZs4E8Z2tY_KLuk7hCMBD1bSPmfEkQ7OtywPhpWoXmrx1JMaen1GNOVOfleg0iIGYMvUdNRvfGTvJePJcWy99QQE2bulsbR-xkGQHKB_x3YaZ19ZHMmT5viz_0GzDeFXZeCzMWU9Z6n6u6Pygj9zQm2vUj0NP9oEqHjr49bBi3F0Py9fm106FWcryDybKsCM8yAojNfdQ/media?maxWidthPx=800&key=AIzaSyBE2cwdc_b0H934UaYurklIc7toYDXhGGQ)


Komfy earns its 4.8 rating the honest way: it's open all day, it's three minutes from Hapjeong Station, and it does not phone in any part of the experience. The truffle butter pizza is wood-fired and genuinely well made, not the kind of pizza that exists only to justify wine orders. The pasta works better than it has any right to in a wine bar setting. Show up in the afternoon and it's a lunch spot. Show up at night and it shifts naturally into something slower, with low lighting and a vinyl record playlist that doesn't feel like decor for its own sake. If you're in Mapo and want somewhere that goes until 1:30am without the energy of a place trying too hard, this is it.

**What to order:** Truffle butter pizza, pasta, wine (let the atmosphere guide you)
**Price:** Budget 70,000–140,000 KRW per person for food and wine
**Getting there:** Hapjeong Station, 3-minute walk
**Hours:** Daily, 12pm–1:30am. Open every day.
**More info:** [bapmap.com/spots/komfy](https://bapmap.com/spots/komfy)

## One Practical Note

Reservations matter more than you'd expect for the smaller spots on this list. Mutin and Sake Shop especially—both are compact rooms where showing up without a booking on a Friday will likely end with you standing outside. Call ahead or check Naver for reservations. For Komfy and Cheolpannam, arriving earlier in the evening gives you more room to settle in. And if you're planning a late push toward 1 or 2am, Sake Shop is your only real option on this list—the others close at midnight.

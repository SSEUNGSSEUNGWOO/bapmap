If you work in Yeoido and want a reliable weekday lunch that takes 45 minutes from seat to departure, ILPUM is the answer. The lunch crowd here moves with the particular efficiency of office workers who know exactly what they're getting. No menus studied at length. No deliberating. They sit down, order in about twelve seconds, and the food arrives fast enough that you can be back at your desk without the whole thing feeling like a project.

That's the environment. Make of it what you will.

## What ILPUM Actually Is

![](https://places.googleapis.com/v1/places/ChIJkUr2dQCffDUR4z2W1Wbkstw/photos/AU_ZVEHYgH23RlQ8kPm_m9PpMkixXfNBxwYi2zy0XJbX5AuRZU2o-gMdEltMmvV5GBGfdT5om_TXlUHnEsj_qJkb6XG5sVqeKDF8c1u1U3DqnwFEkcwhUZ5Yrgm81Jv2qZp4ENZEZINhHTXO2G5tJqb_jt3StQcAj6dCRP2Rsw1imm0jBlah7zIkZDV7NXcWzFtyV5o0PI8ViMYR6vUdKKsUJgY5G_HuK8xJmfqmkKJQnbbnSnZU0dk3lCLd5iZSZ_3FA3rpFjSPU6kF9gGUTYDN7bcLgFf9GmJIR88GBfv7ag9e-8oYhzx9pkzPuh4kMuokjot9DcSliICEG6worG27EN22H8J8U4ZgN7YM0CZrBxA2SKgCV6G429Y1_4vY4wIy-nmwape9GOWIPyvXw5J8VCYJAYc67pSTERQGoUFZElRoqXEB7-6pNZIqwQT8H6fy/media?maxWidthPx=800&key=AIzaSyBE2cwdc_b0H934UaYurklIc7toYDXhGGQ)


ILPUM runs a tight Japanese set-meal format — teishoku (정식) lunches built around grilled fish, sashimi, or katsu, served with rice, miso soup, banchan-adjacent sides, and pickles. It's Japanese in the way that a lot of well-run Korean-Japanese restaurants are: technically accurate, locally calibrated, not trying to convince you you're in Osaka. The restaurant sources seasonal white fish — mackerel, yellow croaker, or daily rotating alternatives depending on availability — which means the grilled fish offering changes subtly throughout the season to reflect what's fresh and reasonably priced at market.

The room is clean and functional. Wooden tables, decent light, no music that asks anything of you.

---

## What to Order (With Prices)

The **생선구이 정식** (Grilled Fish Teishoku) is the anchor dish. Around ₩15,000–₩17,000. A typical order includes the charred seasonal white fish (usually mackerel or yellow croaker), a bowl of rice, miso soup, and three to four rotating side dishes — pickled vegetables, a small egg preparation, and others that change daily. The fish comes charred correctly on the outside, not dried out, with that slightly fatty pull that makes white rice make sense. The miso soup is mild, which I mean neutrally.

The **연어 사시미 정식** (Salmon Sashimi Teishoku) runs closer to ₩22,000–₩25,000 and includes salmon sashimi (typically six to eight pieces depending on the day), rice, miso soup, and the same rotation of four side dishes. The salmon is cold, the cuts are even, and there's enough of it that you won't feel like you ordered a garnish. Nothing about it will surprise you. That's fine.

For something heavier, the **돈카츠 정식** (Tonkatsu Teishoku) holds up. The breading stays crisp, the pork isn't pounded thin enough to taste like nothing. Standard execution, which at this price point is the right benchmark.

Side dishes rotate, but they're consistent in quality — pickled vegetables, egg, small portions that fill the tray without demanding attention.

## Pricing & Service Reality

Lunch runs ₩15,000 to ₩25,000 depending on what you order, and each set includes rice, miso soup, and three to four sides — so the value-per-item is genuine. That's fair for Yeoido, where a lot of Japanese options either cut corners on the fish or charge you for an atmosphere you didn't ask for.

Service is brisk. Friendly enough, not conversational. On busy weekdays, tables turn fast and no one's going to let you sit for forty minutes nursing tea. If you want a slow lunch, come on a Sunday when the pace drops noticeably.

Reservation policy: ILPUM does not appear to take reservations. Weekday lunches between 12:00–1:30 PM fill up. Arrive before noon or after 1:30 to avoid waiting.

---

## The One Thing to Know Before You Go

This is a lunch place that also happens to be open for dinner, not a dinner place with a lunch menu. The energy, the menu, and the whole logic of ILPUM is calibrated to a midday meal. Dinner works — it's quieter, less rushed — but you're not unlocking a different experience. The fish is the same fish.

If you're coming specifically for dinner, manage expectations accordingly.
